About Me


I started in web development in 2014, but my curiosity of the “why” behind products led me to transition into design in 2018.


Since then, I enjoyed taking on challenges of migrating, maintaining, and establishing design systems from scratch, while developing a deep interest in shaping UX governance. This includes setting standards and ensuring coherent experiences across all products to align and enable stakeholders. In a way, putting UX on top of the UX work.


I had the privilege of learning from great colleagues and my design system knowledge has been deepened through blogs by Alice Packard, Diana Mounter, or Nathan Curtis. Outside of work, I also like to connect in Zurich at events like Last Thursday Talks or Friends of Figma.


Thanks to the versatility of UX, I could implement my design system work on products from both B2C and B2B environments, showcased in the following sections of my portfolio.

What I like to read

✕

I enjoy reading across a wide range of topics. Some highlights are shown here:


  • Invisible Women by Caroline C. Perez It made me realize how often design overlooks women’s needs, particularly in health, safety, and efficiency across many study cases.

  • User Friendly by Cliff Kuang A key takeaway for me is understanding how design shapes daily life and the benefits of designing with inclusivity, especially for disabilities.

  • Feel Good Productivity by Ali Abdaal It reminds me to enjoy the journey, rather forcing a specific outcome. Feeling good and staying sincere, and approach work with flexibility leads to real productivity.
